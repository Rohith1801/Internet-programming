India

  • India is the seventh-largest country by land area.
  • It is located in South Asia.
  • India has a diverse culture with over 1,600 spoken languages.
  • The capital city of India is New Delhi.
  • It is known for its rich history and ancient civilization.
  • The national symbol of India is the Bengal Tiger.
  • India gained independence from British rule on August 15, 1947.
  • The Indian flag consists of three horizontal stripes: saffron, white, and green, with a navy blue Ashoka Chakra in the center.
  • India is the world's largest democracy.
  • Mahatma Gandhi, also known as the "Father of the Nation," played a pivotal role in India's independence movement through non-violent civil disobedience.
  • India is famous for its diverse cuisine, including dishes like curry, biryani, and dosa.
  • The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is one of the most famous landmarks in India.
  • India is home to various religions, including Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, and Jainism.
  • Bollywood, based in Mumbai, is one of the largest film industries in the world.
  • The Indian economy is one of the fastest-growing economies globally.
  • India's national sport is field hockey, although cricket is the most popular sport.
  • The Indian rupee is the official currency of India.
  • The Himalayas, the world's highest mountain range, are located in northern India.
  • India is known for its vibrant festivals, including Diwali, Holi, and Eid.
  • The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has made significant advancements in space technology.
